## STEP 2 – RESEARCH & DISCOVERY (Condensed Synthesis)

### 2.1 Market Analysis

**Primary Competitors**

1. **The Lost Book of Herbal Remedies (Apelian / Davis)**  
   - Angle: “Forgotten ancient cures Big Pharma doesn’t want you to know.”  
   - Strengths: Big promise, VSL engine, strong prepper resonance.  
   - Gaps:
     - Less framed as an *organized household system*.
     - Overwhelming for beginners; less “start here if you have X.”

2. **The Home Doctor / Survival MD**  
   - Angle: “What to do when there’s no doctor / during collapse.”  
   - Strengths: Crisis urgency, clear trauma/emergency focus.  
   - Gaps:
     - Narrow: emergencies vs daily wellness.
     - Less herbal, more “field-medicine”; not a cozy, long-term family system.

3. **Mainstream Herbal Bibles (e.g., Rosemary Gladstar, general herbals)**  
   - Angle: “Beautiful encyclopedias of herbs & holistic living.”  
   - Strengths: Aesthetic, credibility, breadth.  
   - Gaps:
     - Often conceptual rather than step-by-step.
     - Less explicit about dosages, interactions, and “when to use what.”

4. **Supplement Brands (Gaia, Organifi, etc.)**  
   - Angle: “We bottle the herbs; just buy our SKUs.”  
   - Strengths: Convenience, trust, strong branding.  
   - Gaps:
     - No empowerment or skill-transfer.
     - Expensive recurring spend vs one-time book.

5. **Low-quality Remedy Compilations / PLR ebooks**  
   - Angle: Cheap “ultimate guide” PDFs.  
   - Gaps:
     - No real expert, no proper safety info, lots of fluff; easy to outclass with trust & rigor.

**Market Gaps / Opportunities**

- A **visually intuitive, beginner-safe home system** that:
  - Organizes by body system & symptom.
  - Clearly lists side effects & interactions.
  - Bridges crisis-prep and everyday use.
- A product that feels like a **physical household asset**, not just “another book.”

**Underserved Segments**

- Mainstream women 40–65 who are health‑savvy but not “preppers.”
- Autoimmune / chronic‑illness communities looking for supportive care.
- Parents & grandparents of sensitive kids.
- Nurses/caregivers who are quietly holistic‑curious.
