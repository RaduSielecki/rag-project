## STEP 2 – RESEARCH & DISCOVERY (Condensed but Actionable)

### 1. Market Analysis

**Representative competitors**

1. **The Lost Book of Herbal Remedies (Apelian & Davis)**  
   - Strengths: huge social proof, strong “hidden remedies” / prepper angle, same author credibility.  
   - Weaknesses: less “organized as a home medicine cabinet” framing, less explicit about side effects & interactions in the positioning.

2. **The Lost Ways / Pioneer Remedy Books**  
   - Strengths: strong scarcity / crisis / “forbidden knowledge” copy, very prepper-friendly.  
   - Weaknesses: vague medical guidance, more “survival lore” than practical everyday home-use system.

3. **Generic “Herbal Remedies PDF” Sellers (AI or anonymous authors)**  
   - Strengths: cheap, instant download, often big lists of “miracle cures.”  
   - Weaknesses: low trust, no credentials, often no contraindications, mostly text-heavy and not visually guided.

4. **Herbal Academy / Online Herbal Schools**  
   - Strengths: depth of knowledge, strong branding, community + courses.  
   - Weaknesses: higher time/financial commitment, not a simple, tangible “do this tonight” solution.

5. **Essential Oils MLMs & “Natural Wellness” Influencers**  
   - Strengths: community, social proof, quick hacks (rollers, diffusers, etc.).  
   - Weaknesses: heavy sales bias toward proprietary products; fewer whole-plant, pantry-based remedies.

**Market gaps & opportunities**

- **Safety transparency gap:** Very few products lead with “side effects, interactions, what NOT to do.”  
- **Physical, crisis-ready gap:** Most are PDFs or web content; very few push “works with no power, no internet, in blackouts.”  
- **Everyday system gap:** Market leans on wild single-remedy hooks vs a calm, navigable “this is your whole home medicine cabinet” system.  
- **Credibility gap:** Many authors are faceless or “coach” level, not biologist + survivalist with chronic illness lived experience.

**Underserved audiences**

- Women 40–65 in **perimenopause/menopause** hell (sleep, anxiety, joint pain, brain fog).  
- **Sandwich generation caregivers** (kids + aging parents).  
- **Medication overload / burnout** sufferers (polypharmacy, side effects, wanting gentler adjunct support).  
- **Budget-pressed families** needing cheaper health care alternatives.  
- Homesteaders who grow food but haven’t yet “closed the loop” on medicine.

## HOW TO USE THIS SYSTEM

- **Meta campaigns:** assign 1–2 angles per campaign, each with creatives tuned to that audience. Test hooks against each other: crisis vs frugal vs hormones vs grandma.
